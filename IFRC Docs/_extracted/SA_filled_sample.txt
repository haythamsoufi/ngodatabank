1. Purpose, Scope and Audience
This document describes the architecture of the solution — what it does, how it is built, and how it fits into the IFRC IT landscape.
1.1 Scope
💡 What is included and what is explicitly out of scope for this solution?
In Scope: Unified federation-wide structured data collection and management: form/template design (Form Builder), assignments to countries/NS and entities, Indicator Bank–linked indicators, focal-point data entry with validation and audit trail, exports (e.g. Excel), REST APIs (including mobile JWT surface), notifications (in-app/email/push where configured), multilingual content, analytics dashboards, and phased AI/RAG document features. MVP alignment: Unified Mid-Year Reporting 2026 and related PMER/FDS processes (FDRS, Unified Planning/Reporting, fed-wide emergencies) per PCH.
Out of Scope: Full one-shot migration of all historical legacy systems and datasets at initial launch; complete sunsetting of Kobo/Excel/Power Platform in one release; full public portal publishing scope unless explicitly required for the reporting round; some advanced analytics/AI integrations may evolve post go-live per PCH and knowledge-transfer notes.
1.2 Audience
💡 Who should read this document?
Primary Readers: DTD enterprise & solution architects; infrastructure and security teams; application support; project manager and PMO; product owner and FDS technical administrators.
Secondary Readers: Business owner and sponsors; National Society and IFRC regional focal points (operational context); senior management consuming federation-wide reporting.
