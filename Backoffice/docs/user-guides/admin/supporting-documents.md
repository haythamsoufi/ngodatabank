# Supporting documents (admin)

Use this guide to understand how supporting documents relate to assignments (for example: evidence files, reports, or attachments).

## What supporting documents are

Supporting documents are files attached to an assignment submission to provide:

- evidence or source documents
- background reports
- spreadsheets used to calculate values

## What to require (keep it minimal)

If you decide to require documents:
- Require the minimum set needed to validate the submission.
- Be explicit: what file, what format, and what naming convention.

Example requirement text:
- “Upload one PDF with the official source for the reported totals (named `Country_Period_Source.pdf`).”

## Where to find documents

Depending on your setup, you may find documents:

- inside an assignment’s detail view, or
- in a “Documents” area for the assignment/submission

## Review documents

When reviewing a submission:

1. Open the submission.
2. Check attached documents exist when required by your process.
3. Confirm the document matches the reported values (sampling is fine if volume is high).

## Handling sensitive data (important)

- Avoid requesting documents with personal identifiers unless explicitly required and approved.
- If sensitive documents are required, agree on:
  - who can access them
  - where they may be exported/shared
  - how long they are retained

## Good practices

- Agree on naming conventions (example: `Country_Period_Source.pdf`).
- Prefer a small set of required documents rather than many optional files.
- Avoid uploading sensitive personal data unless explicitly required and approved.

## Common problems

- **A user can’t upload documents**: confirm they have a role that allows document upload (for example `assignment_documents_uploader` or `assignment_editor_submitter`).
- **Documents are missing**: clarify whether documents are required for that assignment and communicate expectations to focal points.
