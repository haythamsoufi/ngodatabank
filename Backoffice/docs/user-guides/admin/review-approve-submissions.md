# Review and approve submissions

Use this guide if you are responsible for quality checks, approval, or reopening submitted assignments.

## Before you start

- You need an appropriate role (for example **Assignment Approver**).
- Know what “good quality” means for your organization (sources, totals, consistency checks).

## Find submissions to review

1. Open **Admin Panel** → **Form & Data Management** → **Manage Assignments**.
2. Click **Edit** on the assignment you want to review.
3. In the Entity Management section, use the status information (for example **submitted**) to find which countries/entities are ready for review.
4. Click on a country/entity to open the entry form for review.

## Review a submission

When you open a submitted country’s form:

- Check required fields are filled.
- Scan for obvious outliers (unexpectedly high/low values).
- Check internal consistency (totals add up, related fields align).
- Review any comments or notes the focal point added (sources, explanations).

## Approve, reject, or request changes

Depending on what the UI shows in your system, you may have options like:

- **Approve**: confirms the submission is accepted.
- **Reject** (or “Send back”): returns the submission for correction.
- **Reopen**: allows edits after submission/approval (use sparingly and track why).

## When to reopen vs create a new assignment

- **Reopen** when it’s a small fix and you want the same submission to be corrected.
- **Create a new assignment** when the change is large, or the template itself changed, or you need a clear audit trail across periods.

## Tips

- Write short, specific feedback: “Please correct field X (section Y). The total should match Z.”
- Agree in advance on a checklist (3–10 items) so reviews are consistent.

## Common problems

- **I can’t approve**: confirm you have the **Assignment Approver** role.
- **I can’t find submissions**: confirm the assignment is active and countries have completed submission.
- **Data changed after approval**: avoid reopening unless needed; document why you reopened.
