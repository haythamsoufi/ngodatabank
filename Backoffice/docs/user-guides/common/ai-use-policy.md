# AI Use Policy

This document describes the acceptable use, data handling, and responsibilities that apply when using the AI assistant in the IFRC Network Databank Backoffice. Users must acknowledge this policy before using the chatbot (floating widget or immersive view).

## Important notice

**Don't share sensitive information.** We use system traces and telemetry to improve the assistant. Your messages may be processed by external AI providers (e.g. OpenAI, Gemini, Azure).

---

## Purpose

The AI assistant helps you explore data and documents on the platform. It can answer questions about indicators, countries, assignments, and search through uploaded documents. This policy explains acceptable use and important limits.

---

## Acceptable Use

- **Do**: Ask questions about platform data, indicators, and documents.
- **Do**: Use the assistant to compare values, find information, and interpret evidence from reports and plans.
- **Do not**: Share passwords, credentials, or other secrets.
- **Do not**: Paste personal data, financial information, or highly confidential operational details.

---

## Data Handling

Your messages and conversation history may be processed by external AI providers to generate responses. We use system traces and telemetry to improve the assistant.

Technical safeguards include:

- **Data Loss Prevention (DLP)** — Scans that detect and may block or warn about sensitive patterns before sending.
- **PII scrubbing** — Reduces exposure of personal data before content reaches external providers.
- **Audit logging** — Security events are logged (without storing your message content).

These safeguards are best-effort and do not guarantee full removal of sensitive data. Treat the AI assistant as untrusted for sensitive information.

---

## Accuracy and Verification

AI can make mistakes. The assistant may hallucinate or misinterpret data. Always verify important information against the source data or documents before using it for decisions or reporting.

---

## Your Responsibilities

You are responsible for the content you send and how you use the assistant's responses. If you suspect a security incident or accidental exposure of sensitive data, contact your administrator or the platform support team.
